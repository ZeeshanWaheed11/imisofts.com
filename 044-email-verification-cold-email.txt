# Email Verification for Cold Email: Keep Bounce Rates Under 3%

Email verification is the critical step between sourcing your prospect list and sending emails. Sending to invalid addresses damages your domain reputation, increases bounce rates, and reduces deliverability across all future campaigns. This guide covers email verification tools, best practices, timing, and de-duplication strategies to maintain bounce rates below 3%.

## Why Email Verification Matters for Cold Email

Your domain reputation depends directly on bounce rates. High bounce rates (above 5%) signal to mailbox providers that you're sending to invalid addresses, triggering throttling or blocks. Low bounce rates (below 3%) signal healthy list quality and maintain sender reputation.

**Impact of High Bounce Rates**:
- **Reputation Damage**: 5%+ bounce rates harm domain reputation significantly
- **Deliverability Decline**: Yahoo, Outlook, Gmail throttle messages from high-bounce senders
- **Campaign Failure**: Your campaigns go to spam or get bounced entirely
- **Domain Blacklisting**: Persistent high bounce rates can result in blacklisting

**Impact of Low Bounce Rates**:
- **Healthy Reputation**: 2-3% bounce rate maintains good domain reputation
- **Better Deliverability**: Emails go to inbox instead of spam
- **Higher Open Rates**: Inbox placement means people see your emails
- **Sustained Sending**: You can continuously scale without reputation issues

## Email Verification Timing

**When to Verify**:

**Option 1**: Verify immediately after sourcing
- Verify as soon as you export from Apollo
- Removes invalid emails before list organization
- Cleanest approach

**Option 2**: Verify just before sending
- Use if list sourced weeks before sending
- Email decay (people change jobs, companies shut down)
- More realistic bounce rate after time delay

**Best Practice**: Verify immediately after sourcing, then verify again if more than 4 weeks pass before sending.

## Email Verification Best Practices

### Best Practice 1: Verify Immediately After Sourcing

Don't wait. Verify as soon as you export from Apollo.

**Process**:
1. Export from Apollo (20,000 contacts)
2. Download list as CSV
3. Upload to verification tool
4. Wait 1-2 days for batch processing
5. Download verified results
6. Remove invalid emails from your list

### Best Practice 2: De-Duplicate Before Verification

Remove duplicates to avoid paying twice for verification.

**How to De-Duplicate**:
- Use Excel or Google Sheets "Remove Duplicates" feature
- Use specialized deduplication tools (CSV deduplicator)
- Sort by email, manually remove obvious duplicates
- Tools: DupeGuru, Listcleanup, or Peterbe.com deduplicator

**Example**: 20,000 sourced contacts
- Remove obvious duplicates: 19,500 unique emails
- Verify 19,500 emails: Cost £20-50
- Remove bounces (3%): 1,500 removed = 18,000 clean emails

### Best Practice 3: Remove Generic and Test Emails

Don't send to non-human mailboxes:
- info@, support@, hello@, contact@ (company mailboxes)
- test@, demo@, fake@ (test addresses)
- no-reply@, noreply@ (automated addresses)
- Any obvious fake or generic addresses

**How**:
- Use find-and-replace in Excel to identify patterns
- Manually review suspicious addresses
- Remove anything that looks automated

### Best Practice 4: Check Email Format Validity

Before verification, remove obviously invalid formats:
- Missing @ symbol
- Multiple @ symbols
- No domain (example@)
- No local part (@example.com)
- Invalid domain formats

**Tools**: Regex validators can catch obvious format issues

### Best Practice 5: Verify by Domain

Some tools allow verifying by entire domain (check validity of all emails at domain once).

**Advantage**: Saves money if verifying multiple people at same company

**Example**: Verifying 100 people at Microsoft
- Normal verification: 100 × £0.003 = £0.30
- Domain bulk verification: 1 check = £0.01-0.05

## Email Verification Results Interpretation

After verification, you'll get results:

**Valid Email**: Inbox likely exists and email is valid
- Safe to send

**Risky Email**: Email exists but verification uncertain
- Approach: Depends on verification tool confidence score

**Invalid Email**: Email is definitely invalid or doesn't exist
- Remove from list

**Unknown Email**: Verification tool couldn't determine validity
- Approach: Include with caution or remove based on risk appetite

**Catch-All Domain**: Domain accepts all emails
- Risk: Email address may not actually exist
- Approach: Include but monitor bounce rate closely

## Bounce Rate Monitoring

After sending, monitor your actual bounce rates:

**In Instantly, Smartlead, or your email platform**:
- Track bounce rate per domain
- Target: Below 3% bounce rate
- Above 3%: Investigate cause
- Above 5%: Stop sending from that domain, investigate

**Common Causes of High Bounce Rates**:
- List not properly verified
- Email list too old (stale leads)
- Domain reputation issues
- Verification tool caught fewer bad emails than expected

**Corrective Actions**:
- Re-verify list with different verification tool
- Stop sending if bounce rate exceeds 5%
- Check domain reputation (MXToolbox, Google Postmaster Tools)
- Rotate to different domain if reputation damaged

## De-Duplication Deep Dive

**Why De-Duplicate Matters**:
- You'll send multiple emails to same person from different companies (one person may have changed jobs)
- Avoids verification cost duplication
- Cleaner data for CRM integration
- Reduces bounce rates (same email verified once)

**De-Duplication Methods**:

**Method 1: Excel/Google Sheets**
- Copy email column
- Use "Remove Duplicates" feature (Data menu)
- Simple, effective for 10,000-20,000 emails

**Method 2: Specialized Tools**
- DupeGuru (finds exact and near-duplicates)
- listcleanup.com (online deduplicator)
- Peterbe.com deduplicator (advanced options)

**Method 3: Python Script**
- For technical users handling large lists
- Command-line: `sort emails.csv | uniq > dedup.csv`

**Best Practice**: De-duplicate, then verify, then send

## Email Verification Frequency

**Initial Verification**: After sourcing, before first send

**Repeat Verification Timing**:
- If 2 weeks pass: Re-verify before sending
- If 1 month passes: Definitely re-verify (10-15% email decay rate per month)
- If sending to same list again (4+ weeks later): Re-verify
