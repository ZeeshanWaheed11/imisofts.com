# SmartLead Supersearch: How to Find Targeted B2B Leads

SmartLead Supersearch is one of the most powerful lead-finding tools available for B2B outbound. While many teams gravitate toward Apollo, SmartLead Supersearch often delivers superior results, especially for European markets, GDPR-compliant sourcing, and professional email addresses.

At imisofts, we maintain subscriptions to both Apollo and SmartLead Supersearch, and we use each strategically depending on the campaign geography and targeting requirements. This guide covers when and how to use SmartLead Supersearch effectively.

## What is SmartLead Supersearch?

SmartLead Supersearch is the lead-finding component of the SmartLead platform. While SmartLead is primarily known as an email delivery platform (competing with Instantly), their Supersearch feature has evolved into a standalone lead database and search tool.

SmartLead Supersearch has access to approximately 200+ million B2B contacts across 30+ million companies. The search interface lets you filter by job title, company size, industry, location, technologies, hiring signals, and dozens of other parameters.

The key advantage is depth in international markets, particularly Europe. SmartLead was founded in India and has superior data coverage in EU countries compared to Apollo. For agencies and companies targeting Europe, SmartLead Supersearch typically outperforms Apollo.

## SmartLead Supersearch vs Apollo

Both are excellent tools. Here is the real comparison:

**Apollo strengths**:
- Larger overall database (250M+ contacts)
- Superior US and UK coverage
- Better technology stack filtering
- More granular company metadata
- Direct integration with Instantly
- API access for scale operations

**SmartLead Supersearch strengths**:
- Better European coverage (especially Germany, France, Netherlands, Spain, Italy)
- Superior GDPR-compliant sourcing (important for EU campaigns)
- Cleaner professional email addresses (higher percentage of domain emails vs Gmail)
- Better data on smaller European companies
- Integrated with SmartLead platform for seamless sending
- Excellent for GDPR-focused prospecting

**Simple decision rule**:
- Targeting US, UK, or Australia? Use Apollo
- Targeting Europe (any EU country)? Use SmartLead Supersearch
- Targeting globally? Use both — Apollo for English-speaking markets, SmartLead for Europe

For the European lead scraping project we mentioned earlier (26,500+ leads across 30+ countries), we relied heavily on SmartLead Supersearch for lead quality and GDPR compliance.

## How to Use SmartLead Supersearch

### Step 1: Log In and Access Supersearch

SmartLead Supersearch is available if you have a SmartLead account. If you only have a free account, upgrade to access Supersearch (available on paid plans starting at $99/month).

Log in to SmartLead, go to "Leads" section, and click "Supersearch."

### Step 2: Build Your Search Filters

Supersearch search interface is organized by filter categories. Here are the most important ones:

**Job Title Filters**:
- Search for specific titles: "VP of Marketing," "CMO," "Marketing Manager"
- Use title synonyms: Include variations like "Chief Marketing Officer" if searching for CMOs
- Exclude titles: Remove support staff, interns, or irrelevant titles

For example, if targeting marketing leaders, search for: VP of Marketing, CMO, Chief Marketing Officer, Director of Marketing, Head of Marketing. Exclude: Marketing Assistant, Marketing Coordinator, Marketing Intern.

**Company Filters**:
- Company size: Select headcount ranges (10-50, 50-500, 500+)
- Company revenue: Annual revenue ranges (if available in your target region)
- Company industry: Use industry categories (SaaS, e-commerce, financial services, etc.)
- Company growth: Hiring signals, recent funding (when available)

**Location Filters**:
- Country: Select specific countries (Germany, UK, France, Netherlands, etc.)
- Region/state: For larger countries, narrow to specific regions
- Multiple locations: Select multiple countries if doing international outreach

**Technology Filters**:
- Company technologies: Some data on technology stack (varies by region)
- Email verification: Filter for verified vs unverified emails

**Other Filters**:
- LinkedIn presence: Filter for contacts with LinkedIn profiles
- Email confidence: Filter by email verification level (high, medium, low)
- Recent updates: Leads updated in last 30/60/90 days (more likely to be accurate)

### Step 3: Set Search Parameters for Your ICP

Example search for European marketing leaders:

- **Titles**: VP of Marketing, CMO, Director of Marketing, Head of Marketing
- **Countries**: Germany, France, UK, Netherlands, Belgium
- **Company size**: 50-500 employees
- **Industry**: B2B SaaS, Technology, Marketing Services
- **Email verification**: High or Medium confidence
- **LinkedIn**: Present

This search should return 5,000-15,000 results depending on how restrictive your filters are.

### Step 4: Export Your Leads

Supersearch allows exporting results to CSV. Export includes columns for:
- First name
- Last name
- Email
- Company name
- Job title
- Company size
- Country
- LinkedIn URL (if available)
- Any other data fields you selected

Export to CSV and save locally.

### Step 5: Enrich and Process Your List

After exporting from Supersearch, follow the same process as with Apollo leads:

1. **Deduplicate** using email as primary key
2. **Enrich with Clay** to add missing data (phone numbers, funding, technographics)
3. **Verify emails** using verification tools
4. **Segment** by country, company size, or other factors
5. **Import into SmartLead** for campaign execution

## Email Quality in SmartLead Supersearch

One major advantage of SmartLead Supersearch is email quality. SmartLead reports a higher percentage of professional (domain-based) email addresses compared to Apollo.

**What this means**:
- Professional emails: john@acmetech.com (higher deliverability)
- Consumer emails: john.smith@gmail.com (lower deliverability for B2B cold email)

For every 100 leads from Supersearch, expect approximately:
- 70-80% professional domain emails
- 20-30% consumer email addresses (Gmail, Yahoo, Outlook)

Professional emails have significantly better cold email deliverability (typically 60-85% inbox placement vs 40-60% for consumer emails).

## GDPR Compliance in SmartLead Supersearch

SmartLead Supersearch is particularly strong for GDPR compliance. When sourcing leads in the EU, GDPR compliance is critical — non-compliance can result in fines and legal issues.

SmartLead Supersearch helps with GDPR compliance by:
- Sourcing only from publicly available data (LinkedIn, company websites, directories)
- Verifying legitimate business purpose for outreach
- Providing audit trails for data sourcing
- Supporting opt-out requests and list management

When sourcing in Europe with SmartLead Supersearch, you get leads that are legally sourced and defensible for GDPR purposes. This is not a substitute for legal review, but it significantly reduces compliance risk compared to buying lists from unclear sources.

FAQ_SCHEMA:
Q: How does SmartLead Supersearch compare to Apollo for European leads?
A: SmartLead Supersearch typically has 20-30% more European leads and higher-quality professional email addresses. If you are targeting Europe, Supersearch is usually the better choice.

Q: Can I use Supersearch without sending through SmartLead?
A: Yes, you can export leads to CSV and use them with any email platform. However, if you are also sending through SmartLead, the integration is seamless and efficient.

Q: How many leads should I export from a single search?
A: Depends on your ICP specificity. For well-defined ICPs, 5,000-10,000 leads is typical. Broader searches can yield 20,000+. Remember that quality matters more than quantity.

Q: Is Supersearch data GDPR-compliant?
A: Supersearch sources only from publicly available data, which supports GDPR compliance. However, you must still comply with GDPR requirements when sending. Work with legal counsel on your specific situation.

Q: Should I use Supersearch or Apollo?
A: Use Supersearch for Europe, Australia, or other international markets. Use Apollo for US and English-speaking markets. Many teams maintain both for comprehensive global coverage.
