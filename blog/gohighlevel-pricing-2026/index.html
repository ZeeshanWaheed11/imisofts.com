Quick answer: GoHighLevel has three self-serve plans in 2026: Starter at $97 per month, Unlimited at $297, and Agency Pro at $497, plus a quote-based Enterprise tier. Most agencies belong on Unlimited at $297, which unlocks unlimited client sub-accounts, API access, and at-cost rebilling. A solo operator does fine on Starter. Take Agency Pro only when you are ready to resell the platform under your own brand. Budget for usage on top: email at $0.675 per 1,000 sends, SMS at roughly $0.0079 per segment in the US, and AI on one of three modes (pay per use, $50 Growth, or $97 Unlimited, each per sub-account). For most users the extras land between $20 and $150 per month.

The three sticker prices have not moved all year. The AI and usage layer underneath them has, and that is the part that decides your real bill. Every number below was re-verified against HighLevel's own pricing page and billing docs on July 15, 2026. Two figures we published in June are now wrong, and both were costing readers money, so they are corrected in full below. For features rather than invoices, read our honest GoHighLevel review.

GoHighLevel pricing at a glance

  • Starter, $97 per month or $970 per year: the full platform, unlimited contacts and users, capped at 3 sub-accounts. No API access, no rebilling.
  • Unlimited, $297 per month or $2,970 per year: adds unlimited sub-accounts, basic API access, a white-labelled desktop app, and rebilling of phone and email usage to clients at cost.
  • Agency Pro, $497 per month or $4,970 per year: adds SaaS Mode, automated sub-account creation, rebilling with your own markup, and advanced API access.
  • Enterprise, quote only: the fourth tier most guides skip. Bundles a dedicated CSM, custom development, premium support, higher API limits, and the white-label mobile app and HIPAA compliance included rather than billed as add-ons.
  • Annual billing: exactly ten months for twelve, about 17 percent off. That is near $81 per month on Starter, $248 on Unlimited, $414 on Agency Pro.
  • Free trial: 14 days on every plan, card required. Usage-based telecom fees can still be charged during it.

The subscription line will not surprise you. The surprises live in the usage wallet, the AI plans, and the reselling menu.

What changed in HighLevel pricing recently

Pricing guides rot quietly. Here is what moved since our June version. Two are corrections to what this page previously told you, and we would rather say so than silently edit them out.

  • AI Employee is now three plans, not two. A new Growth tier sits at $50 per month per sub-account, between pay per use and the $97 Unlimited plan. For light AI volume that is a real saving that did not exist before.
  • Voice AI was unbundled on May 20, 2026. The single blended per-minute rate is gone; it is now a stack of components. Correction: this page previously quoted roughly $0.163 per minute as a blended average. That number is historical and should not be used for budgeting.
  • AI Employee Unlimited now covers outbound Voice AI and the widget. Correction: we previously said only inbound was included and that outbound and the widget still billed pay per use. HighLevel's current AI pricing doc lists Voice AI as unlimited across inbound, outbound and widget on the $97 plan. If you avoided outbound AI calls because of the old rule, that constraint is gone. Phone charges still apply to the call.
  • Ad Manager dropped to $0 per month for agencies, with no platform fee, and you can still resell it at any price. Client ad spend is separate.
  • Ask AI and AI Studio are free until September 1, 2026 under the Summer of AI promotion. Token charges apply after that, so anything you build on Ask AI this summer has a price tag arriving in the autumn.
  • Agent Studio is still excluded from every plan and stays pay per use. Unchanged, and the exclusion people most often miss.

Starter at $97: right for one business, tight for an agency

Starter is the whole product, not a crippled version: CRM, funnels, automations, calendars, reputation management, courses, unlimited contacts and users. The single wall is 3 sub-accounts, meaning your agency account plus two clients. There is no API access and no rebilling, so any SMS, email or AI your clients burn comes out of your wallet and stays there. Right for a single business, a consultant with one or two retainers, or anyone learning the platform before betting an agency on it.

Unlimited at $297: the agency default

For $200 more you get unlimited sub-accounts, which is the whole point: your software cost stops scaling with your client count. Add your fortieth client and the bill is still $297. You also get basic API access, white-label branding on the desktop app, and rebilling at cost. Turn rebilling on the day you upgrade; agencies that leave it off are quietly subsidising their clients' text messages. Bill two clients $300 or more and the plan has paid for itself. Agency Pro at $497: SaaS Mode and markup money

Agency Pro exists to let you sell GoHighLevel as your own software: publish your own plans, take Stripe payments, and have client accounts provision themselves. The number that pays for it is rebilling with markup. On Unlimited you pass usage through at cost; on Agency Pro you set the client price above cost and keep the difference. AI rebilling in particular requires this plan. Ten SaaS customers at $197 is $1,970 recurring against a $497 bill. Full setup in our SaaS Mode guide. Do not buy it for features. Buy it when you have an offer ready to launch.

The usage costs nobody puts on the pricing page

Phone and email run through a prepaid Agency Wallet that auto-recharges your card. Unit prices are small, volumes are not. Per HighLevel's billing guide, current in July 2026:

  • Email: $0.675 per 1,000 sends on every plan. A 100,000-send month is about $67.50.
  • SMS and calls: Twilio-parity rates, roughly $0.0079 per segment and $0.014 per minute outbound in the US, plus a couple of dollars per number per month. Long texts split into segments, so a 300-character blast counts two or three times.
  • One genuinely good change: HighLevel removed the 5 percent Stripe markup it used to add to phone charges. Since October 1, 2025 phone usage bills at exact carrier passthrough with no uplift line.
  • Premium workflow actions: $0.01 per execution. Heavy users should buy a Workflow Pro tier: $10 for 10,000 executions, $25 for 30,000, $50 for 65,000. ChatGPT and Workflow AI actions are billed separately and not covered.
  • Email validation: $2.50 per 1,000 checks, worth it to protect deliverability.

Then the reselling menu, priced per enabled sub-account unless noted. These are the lines that turn a $297 plan into a $600 one without anyone deciding to spend more: SEO by Search Atlas $79, dedicated sending IP $59 (on $297 and $497 plans only), branded client portal app $49, online listings $30 monthly or $300 per year, premium prospecting $29, WhatsApp $10 plus per-conversation usage, WordPress hosting $10 for one site up to $497 unlimited. At agency level: HIPAA compliance $297 per month (once enabled it cannot be deactivated), white-label mobile app $497, premium support $500, certification $97, and a one-off $1,000 advanced account setup.

Realistic totals: a light single-business user adds $10 to $30 per month. A busy agency adds $70 to $150 or more before add-ons. On $297 and up, rebill it from day one.

AI pricing in 2026: three modes, not two

The AI suite is priced separately from the plans, and there are now three billing modes, each set per enabled sub-account.

1. Pay per use. No monthly fee. Conversation AI bills tokens at model rates, for example GPT-5 at $1.25 per million input and $10 per million output, or GPT-5 Mini at $0.25 and $2.00. Reviews AI is $0.01 per review, Content AI $0.063 per image and $0.0945 per 1,000 words. The default for occasional use.

2. AI Employee Growth, $50 per month. The new middle tier, and the one that will save most small agencies money. Includes 1,000 Conversation AI responses, 100 Voice AI minutes covering inbound, outbound and widget combined, unlimited Reviews and Content AI, and included Ask AI and AI Studio usage. Go over and the excess bills at pay-per-use rates automatically; access is not cut off.

3. AI Employee Unlimited, $97 per month. Unlimited Conversation AI, Voice AI across all three modes, Reviews AI and Content AI, subject to a fair use policy. Ask AI and AI Studio get 3x the Growth allowance rather than true unlimited, measured in 5-hour windows.

Voice AI is the number to watch. Since May 20, 2026 it is a stack, not a rate: Voice Engine at $0.045 per minute, plus text-to-speech, plus model tokens, plus the phone call. The TTS choice alone swings the bill more than 10x. OpenAI and Cartesia are $0.015 per minute, ElevenLabs V2.5 is $0.035, ElevenLabs V3 is $0.170. A 10-minute call on OpenAI TTS is about $0.60 in voice cost before tokens and phone charges. On pay per use, choose the voice deliberately.

The exclusions that catch people. Agent Studio is in no plan and stays pay per use, billing tokens plus $0.01 per web search. Phone charges always apply to voice calls whatever your AI plan. And the per-sub-account structure is the real agency trap: Unlimited on ten client accounts is $970 per month, not $97.

The practical rule: light accounts on pay per use, steady accounts on Growth at $50, and only move an account to Unlimited once its own usage genuinely exceeds that. Then rebill at markup if you are on Agency Pro. Used that way, an AI receptionist that books a handful of missed-call leads is one of the easier upsells in our AI automation work.

Three realistic monthly bills

  • Solo consultant, Starter: $97 plan, light SMS and email around $15, no paid AI plan. About $112 per month, replacing $200 or more in separate tools.
  • Agency with 10 clients, Unlimited: $297 plan, $100 to $200 of usage largely rebilled at cost, AI on three sub-accounts. That was $291 at $97 each in June; on Growth at $50 the same three are $150, so an identical setup is $141 per month cheaper than it was five weeks ago.
  • SaaS reseller, Agency Pro: $497 plan, usage and AI resold at markup, ten customers at $197 equals $1,970 recurring.

How to pay less

  • Use the new $50 Growth tier. If a sub-account is not burning 1,000 AI responses or 100 Voice AI minutes a month, Unlimited at $97 is nearly double what you need. The biggest saving added to GoHighLevel pricing this year.
  • Pick a cheap TTS voice. Moving Voice AI from ElevenLabs V3 to OpenAI or Cartesia saves $0.155 per minute. Over 500 minutes that is $77.50 a month for a difference most callers will not register.
  • Go annual once you are sure: ten months for twelve.
  • Start on Starter, upgrade when client three arrives. Upgrades are instant and prorated.
  • Turn on rebilling the day you hit Unlimited.
  • Buy a Workflow Pro tier before a big automation push. 30,000 premium actions is $300 pay-as-you-go and $25 on the Growth tier.
  • Take online listings annually at $300 rather than $30 monthly if the client is staying.
  • Review the wallet weekly under Agency Settings, then Billing. The AI Suite breaks AI spend down by location and model.

Which plan should you pick in 2026?

Pick Starter at $97 if you are one business or an agency with up to three clients and no API needs. Pick Unlimited at $297 if you run or plan to run an agency; it is the best value of the three. Pick Agency Pro at $497 only when you are committed to reselling, where markup rebilling turns the price into a margin engine. Consider Enterprise only if you already buy the white-label mobile app, HIPAA and premium support separately, since that stack alone is over $1,200 per month.

The honest bottom line: the sticker prices are fair for what consolidates into one login, and the base plans have not risen all year. But your real bill is plan plus usage plus AI plus add-ons, and the gap between a $120 month and a $500 month is configuration, not the plan you picked. The AI layer has now been repriced twice in 2026, so whatever any guide tells you, this one included, check the live number in your own billing screen before committing a client to it.
