How to Use These Subject Lines (2025 Rules)

Three quick rules for cold email subject lines that actually work:

  • No clickbait. If your subject says "quick question," ask a quick question in the email.
  • Skip Re: and Fwd: These fake reply tricks hurt trust and trigger spam filters.
  • Keep personalization in the body. {{firstName}} in subjects looks automated. Put it in the first line instead.

Ideal length: 2-6 words or 18-48 characters. Mobile cuts off longer subjects. Shorter gets opened.

A/B Testing Plan (Instantly/Smartlead)

Step-by-step testing process:

  1. Pick 2 intents (e.g., Benefit vs Curiosity)
  2. Create 2 variants for each (4 total subject lines)
  3. Assign 200 contacts per variant (800 total)
  4. Run for 3-5 days
  5. Measure reply rate (not just opens)
  6. Promote winner to full campaign
  7. Rotate new test weekly

Weekly Testing Schedule

Week Test A Test B Contacts
Week 1 Benefit: "Double your demos" Curiosity: "Strange request" 400 each
Week 2 Problem: "Pipeline dried up?" Proof: "{{competitor}} results" 400 each
Week 3 Winner from Week 1 Shortcut: "10-minute setup" 400 each
Week 4 Overall winner New challenger 400 each

Important: Match email body to subject promise. If subject says "quick question," first line must be a question. Keep body copy consistent across tests.

Deliverability Notes

Keep these rules to stay out of spam:

  • Align subject with body. Mismatch triggers spam filters.
  • Avoid Re:/Fwd: and ALL CAPS. Dead giveaways for mass email.
  • One promise per subject. "Save time AND money" looks spammy.
  • Numbers must match. If subject says "3 tips," body needs exactly 3 tips.
  • Easy opt-out. Add "Let me know if this isn't relevant" in footer.
  • Modest volume during tests. Test with 200 per variant, not 2000.

FAQs

How long should a cold email subject line be?

Keep it between 2-6 words or 18-48 characters. Mobile screens cut off longer subjects. Shorter performs better in our tests.

Is using emojis okay in B2B cold emails?

Skip emojis for first touch. They trigger spam filters and look unprofessional in B2B. Save them for follow-ups after a reply.

Should I personalize the subject line?

No. Keep personalization in the email body's first line. Subject lines with {{firstName}} look automated and hurt open rates.

How many subject line tests should I run per week?

Test 2-4 subject lines weekly with 200 contacts per variant. More testing means less volume for proven winners. Rotate winners every 2 weeks.
