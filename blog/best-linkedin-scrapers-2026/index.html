Quick answer: if you want LinkedIn leads at scale without risking your own account, use a cloud scraper that does not need your login cookie. Apify is the best all round pick in 2026 (pay per result, roughly $4 per 1,000 profiles or $10 per 1,000 with an email search, no cookies required). Evaboot is the cleanest option if you already live inside Sales Navigator. Bright Data is the cheapest per record if you are buying data in bulk. PhantomBuster and Wiza are faster to set up but they run through your LinkedIn session, which is exactly where account bans come from.

Below: what each tool costs, what it is genuinely best at, the catch nobody puts in the headline, and the legal reality in 2026 after LinkedIn sued the biggest scraping company in the market out of existence.

The three types of LinkedIn scraper (pick the type first, then the tool)

Almost every "best LinkedIn scraper" list mixes three very different products together. They carry very different risk:

  • Browser extensions and session based tools (PhantomBuster, Wiza, Evaboot). They act as you, using your logged in LinkedIn session. Fast to start, but LinkedIn can rate limit or restrict the account they see doing the scraping: yours.
  • Cloud scrapers and APIs (Apify Actors, Bright Data scraper APIs). They run on someone else infrastructure and, in the best case, need no cookie and no LinkedIn account at all. Your account is never in the blast radius.
  • Pre collected datasets (Bright Data LinkedIn datasets). You are not scraping anything. You are buying a file of already collected public profile data.

The right choice depends on volume, whether you need verified emails, and how much account risk you can carry. If you want the wider category view, see our best web scraping tools roundup and the best Google Maps scrapers guide for local business data.

  • Apify: best overall for lead generation. Pay per result, no cookies needed, from $4 per 1,000 profiles. Platform plans start free, then $29 per month.

1. Apify: best overall LinkedIn scraper for lead generation

Apify is a cloud platform with a store of ready made scrapers called Actors. For LinkedIn there is not one tool, there is a shelf of them, and you pay only for the results you pull.

Checked live on the Apify Store in July 2026, the strongest LinkedIn Actors are:

  • LinkedIn Profile Search Scraper (harvestapi, 4.75 stars from 78 reviews, 99.3 percent success rate, 28,000+ users): search LinkedIn with filters and pull matching profiles. $0.10 per search page of up to 25 profiles, $4 per 1,000 full profiles, $10 per 1,000 profiles with an email search.
  • LinkedIn Profile Scraper (harvestapi, 49,000+ users): bulk enrich a list of profile URLs. Same $4 and $10 per 1,000 pricing.
  • LinkedIn Company Employees Scraper (harvestapi): pull every employee at a target account. $3 per 1,000 basic profiles, $8 full, $12 with email search.
  • LinkedIn Jobs Scraper (curious_coder, 115,000+ users): $1 per 1,000 job results. This is the cheapest hiring signal on the market, and hiring is a buying trigger.

Why it wins: the best of these Actors run without cookies and without a LinkedIn account, so your profile is never the one making the requests. Pricing is per result, so 1,000 leads with an email search costs about $10 in usage rather than a subscription you have to grow into. Actors are maintained by their developers, so when LinkedIn changes its markup you are not the one fixing a broken script.

The catch: it is a platform, not a one click button. You will spend an hour learning inputs, and Actor quality varies. One popular LinkedIn Actor has 61,000 users but only a 3.52 star rating, which tells you to read the reviews before you trust an Actor with a real campaign. Apify usage also sits on top of a plan: free to start with $5 of monthly usage, then $29 per month for Starter. Unused prepaid credit does not roll over.

Try Apify free Pay per result, run it in the cloud, keep your LinkedIn account out of it. Free plan, no card needed.

2. Evaboot: best for clean Sales Navigator exports

Evaboot is a Chrome extension that exports a Sales Navigator search or list to a clean CSV, then finds and verifies work emails. Verified on evaboot.com in July 2026: one plan, credit based, starting at $9 per month for 100 credits and scaling to 200,000 credits. One exported lead costs 1 credit, one found and verified email costs 1 credit, so a lead with a verified email is 2 credits. Credits roll over while your subscription is active, seats are unlimited, and yearly billing gives you two months free. 4.6 stars, 200,000+ users.

Why it is good: it does the one thing Sales Navigator refuses to do, and it cleans the results. Sales Navigator search filters are notoriously leaky, so Evaboot re checks every lead against your filters and flags the ones that do not actually match. That single feature saves a lot of wasted sends.

The catch: it only works with LinkedIn Sales Navigator, so the real price is $9 plus $119.99 per month for a Sales Navigator Core seat. It runs through your session, and it caps your daily exports precisely because LinkedIn limits accounts that pull too much too fast.

3. PhantomBuster: best for no code multi step automation

PhantomBuster is not just a scraper. It is a library of 50+ automations ("Phantoms") that chain together: scrape a search, extract profiles, auto connect, follow up, export to a CRM. Published 2026 tiers are Starter $69 per month (20 execution hours, 5 Phantom slots, 500 email credits), Pro $159 (80 hours, 15 slots) and Team $439 (300 hours, 50 slots, 10,000 email credits), with roughly 20 percent off on annual billing. PhantomBuster renders its pricing page in JavaScript, so treat these as the widely reported tiers rather than a figure we pulled off a static page.

Why it is good: nothing else makes multi step LinkedIn workflows this approachable without code.

The catch: you are billed in execution hours, not results, so a slow or badly configured Phantom quietly burns your plan. And it runs on your LinkedIn session cookie. If LinkedIn decides your account is behaving like a bot, the account that pays the price is yours, not PhantomBuster's.

4. Wiza: best for quick exports that include phone numbers

Wiza turns a Sales Navigator list into a contact export with emails and direct dials. Reported 2026 pricing: a free tier (a small monthly credit allowance), Starter $49 per month for 100 credits, Email $99 for 500 emails and Email plus Phone $199 for 500 of each, per user. A valid email costs 1 credit, a phone number costs 5.

Why it is good: phone numbers. If your motion is call plus email, pulling both in one pass is worth paying for. Pair it with our best cold calling software comparison.

The catch: per user pricing plus a Sales Navigator seat makes it the most expensive way to build a 1,000 lead list, and phone credits burn 5x faster than email credits.

5. Bright Data: best for bulk LinkedIn data at enterprise scale

Bright Data sells the data instead of the scraping. Verified on brightdata.com in July 2026, its LinkedIn People Profiles dataset holds 671M+ records across 42 fields, priced from $250 per 100,000 records (up to $0.0025 per record) with a $250 minimum order, delivered as JSON, CSV or Parquet to S3, Snowflake, GCS, Azure or SFTP. Refresh subscriptions cut the price further. Bright Data also sells company, job listing and post datasets, plus scraper APIs from $0.75 per 1,000 records.

Why it is good: nothing beats it on unit cost at volume, there is no scraping infrastructure to babysit, and it carries a GDPR ready posture with SOC and ISO certifications, which matters if procurement is involved.

The catch: a $250 floor, no verified work emails in the profile dataset (you still need enrichment), and a bulk snapshot is by definition less fresh than a live pull. Overkill for anyone who needs 500 leads a month.

6. Sales Navigator alone: the compliant baseline

The honest option nobody sells you: use Sales Navigator as intended. Verified on LinkedIn's own pricing page (updated March 2026): Core $119.99 per month or $1,079.88 per year, Advanced $159.99 per month, Advanced Plus by quote. Every plan includes 50 InMails per month and 50+ search filters.

The catch: there is no export. You cannot get your list into a CSV, a CRM or a sequencer without a third party tool, which is why this entire category exists. For low volume, high value accounts (10 to 30 targets a quarter), Sales Navigator plus manual research plus InMail is genuinely the best answer, and it carries zero terms of service risk.

What LinkedIn scraping actually costs per 1,000 leads

Sticker price is not the bill. Here is the honest math for 1,000 leads with a verified work email, the only unit that matters for outbound:

  • Apify: about $10 in usage ($10 per 1,000 profiles with email search), inside a $29 per month Starter plan. No Sales Navigator needed. Cheapest real path.
  • Evaboot: 2,000 credits (1 export + 1 email each). At the entry tier that is roughly $0.09 per credit, so about $180, and the per credit rate drops on larger tiers. Add $119.99 for Sales Navigator.
  • Wiza: around $199 per month on the Email plan gets you 500 emails, so 1,000 costs two months or a bigger plan. Add Sales Navigator.
  • PhantomBuster: $69 per month buys 20 execution hours and only 500 email credits, so 1,000 enriched leads pushes you toward the $159 tier. Add Sales Navigator.
  • Bright Data: $250 minimum buys 100,000 profile records, but with no verified emails, so budget enrichment on top.

The pattern: cookie based tools charge you like software and force a $119.99 Sales Navigator seat on top. Cloud tools charge you like infrastructure. At any real volume, infrastructure wins.

Is scraping LinkedIn legal in 2026?

This is not legal advice, and you should ask a lawyer about your jurisdiction. But two cases define the landscape, and every honest roundup should tell you about them.

hiQ Labs v. LinkedIn. The Ninth Circuit held in April 2022 that scraping public data is unlikely to violate the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act. Scrapers celebrated. Then in November 2022 the district court held that hiQ had breached LinkedIn's User Agreement anyway, and the parties settled in December 2022 with a permanent injunction, an order to delete the scraped data and code, and $500,000 in damages. Public does not mean permitted.

LinkedIn v. Proxycurl. In January 2025 LinkedIn sued Nubela, the company behind Proxycurl, the most popular LinkedIn data API on the market, alleging it ran hundreds of thousands of fake accounts to harvest member data. The case settled in mid 2025 and Proxycurl shut down in July 2025, at roughly $10M in annual recurring revenue. The founder's own goodbye post said there was no winning against a plaintiff with Microsoft's legal budget.

What that means in practice for a small team doing outbound:

  • Scraping public LinkedIn data is a breach of LinkedIn's User Agreement, not automatically a crime. The realistic penalty for a normal user is losing the account, not a lawsuit.
  • Fake accounts are the line that gets you sued. Both hiQ and Proxycurl created or bought accounts to get access. If you are reselling scraped LinkedIn data as a product, you are in the category LinkedIn actually litigates.
  • If you contact people in the EU or the UK, profile data is personal data under GDPR. You need a lawful basis, an accurate privacy notice and a working opt out, whatever tool collected it.

How to not get your LinkedIn account restricted

  • Prefer no cookie cloud scrapers for anything above a few hundred profiles. If the tool never touches your session, your account cannot be the one flagged.
  • Never create fake profiles. That is the behaviour LinkedIn sues over, and it is the fastest permanent ban there is.
  • Respect daily volume. Cookie based tools that impose export caps are protecting you, not limiting you. Turn the caps off and you become the anomaly in LinkedIn's logs.
  • Verify before you send. Scraped emails are guesses until a verifier says otherwise. Bouncing a scraped list is how you burn the sending domain you just paid to warm up.

From scraped profiles to booked meetings

A LinkedIn scraper produces a CSV. A CSV is not pipeline. The chain that actually books meetings looks like this: define the ICP in Sales Navigator filters, extract with a cloud scraper, enrich and verify the emails, split the list by segment, then send from warmed domains with a message written for that segment, and follow up on LinkedIn only where it adds signal.

Verdict: which LinkedIn scraper should you use in 2026?

  • Building outbound lists at any real volume: Apify. Per result pricing, no cookies, no Sales Navigator dependency.
  • Already living inside Sales Navigator: Evaboot. Cleanest exports, honest credit pricing, useful filter checking.
  • Need connection requests and follow ups too: PhantomBuster, with your eyes open about account risk.
  • Calling as well as emailing: Wiza, for the direct dials.
  • Buying data by the million: Bright Data.
  • Chasing 20 enterprise accounts: just use Sales Navigator properly and skip this whole category.

The tool matters less than the discipline around it. A clean 500 lead list sent well beats a scraped 50,000 row dump every single time, and it will not get your account restricted.
