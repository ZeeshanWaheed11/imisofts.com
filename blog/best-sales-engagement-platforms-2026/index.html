What a sales engagement platform actually is

A sales engagement platform (SEP) is the system your reps work out of all day: it sequences emails, calls, LinkedIn touches, and tasks into multi-step cadences, tracks every interaction, and reports on what converts. Think of it as the execution layer that sits on top of your CRM.

It is not the same thing as cold email software, which focuses on high-volume sending, inbox rotation, and deliverability (we compared those separately in our best cold email software guide). And it is not a data or enrichment tool, which is about finding and completing contact records (covered in our lead enrichment tools comparison). Most teams end up pairing an SEP with one or both. The exception is Apollo, which is the main reason it keeps winning deals it has no business winning at its price.

1. Apollo: best all-in-one value

Apollo is the only platform on this list that ships the prospect database and the engagement layer in one product: 275M+ contacts, email sequences, a built-in dialer, LinkedIn tasks, intent signals, and a light CRM. Everyone else on this list expects you to bring your own data. That consolidation is why a 10-seat Apollo team pays around $490 per month while a comparable Salesloft contract runs well past $1,200 before you add a data vendor.

Pricing is public and starts free: the Basic plan is $49 per user per month billed annually, Professional is $79, and Organization is $119 ($59, $99, and $149 monthly). The free tier includes working sequences, which makes it the cheapest way in the industry to prove outbound works before spending anything. Users rate it 4.7/5 across more than 9,000 reviews cited on its own site. We wrote up the full economics in our Apollo ROI breakdown and Apollo review.

The catch: credits and coverage. Email addresses are effectively unlimited under a fair-use cap, so the pools you actually meter are mobile-number and export credits, and a mobile reveal burns 5 to 8 times faster than an email. Those metered credits reset monthly, and data accuracy is strongest in the US (independent tests put it around 85 to 90 percent there, lower in Europe and APAC). Verify lists before you send and plan for some bounce.

2. Outreach: the enterprise standard

Outreach has the deepest sequencing, forecasting, and conversation intelligence stack in the category, and it is built for large teams with dedicated RevOps. Pricing is fully quote-based with mandatory annual contracts: buyers report roughly $120 to $175 per user per month across the Amplify Core, Plus, and Pro tiers, plus implementation fees that commonly run $5,000 to $25,000 and annual platform fees on top. In 2026 it also added a consumption-based AI credit layer, so total spend is harder to predict.

The catch: no free plan, no public pricing, and no native database, so you still need a data vendor and an ops person before your first sequence sends. G2 users score it 4.3/5, with cost the most common complaint.

3. Salesloft: best cadence analytics and coaching

Salesloft is Outreach's closest rival and arguably ahead on rep coaching, call recording, and cadence analytics, with Drift folded in for inbound conversations. Since completing its merger with Clari in December 2025, it is increasingly packaged alongside Clari's forecasting stack, so expect a broader "revenue platform" pitch in 2026 sales conversations. It rates 4.5/5 on G2 across roughly 3,900 reviews. Like Outreach, everything is a custom quote: list pricing typically lands between $125 and $165 per user per month, the Essentials tier starts lower, and most mid-market buyers negotiate to $100 to $130.

The catch: add-ons stack fast. The dialer is commonly quoted at $300 to $400 per user per year and Conversations can add 20 to 40 percent to the platform cost, on top of a separate data subscription.

4. Reply.io: best mid-market multichannel with an AI SDR

Reply.io covers email, LinkedIn, calls, SMS, and WhatsApp in one sequence builder, and its Jason AI agent can run prospecting semi-autonomously. The Multichannel plan is $89 per user per month billed annually, an email-only plan starts around $49 to $59, and Ultimate runs about $139. Jason AI is a separate product starting at $500 per month.

The catch: the sticker price is not the real price. LinkedIn automation costs about $69 per account per month extra and calls/SMS add another $29, so true multichannel usage typically lands at $150 to $200 per user per month.

5. HubSpot Sales Hub: best if you already run HubSpot CRM

If your company lives in HubSpot, Sales Hub is the path of least resistance: sequences, tasks, calling, and reporting native to the CRM your data already sits in. Starter is about $15 per seat per month on annual billing, but sequences and automation are gated at Professional, which is $90 per seat annually ($100 monthly) plus a one-time $1,500 onboarding fee. HubSpot dropped its old five-seat minimum for Sales Hub in 2024, so a single rep can now start on Professional for about $90 per month plus onboarding, rather than the roughly $450 the five-seat floor used to force.

The catch: you are buying an ecosystem, not just an SEP. Costs compound as you add seats and hubs, and there is no native prospect database, so budget for data separately.

6. Salesforce Sales Engagement: best Salesforce-native cadences

For Salesforce shops, Sales Engagement adds cadences, automated task queues, Einstein Activity Capture, and conversation insights directly inside Sales Cloud for $50 per user per month, and it is bundled into the Unlimited and Agentforce 1 Sales editions. Reps never leave the CRM, and admins keep one security and reporting model.

The catch: it only makes sense on top of Sales Cloud, and Salesforce add-ons stack: Inbox, Conversation Insights, and premium support can roughly double the effective per-seat cost by the time the stack is complete.

7. Klenty: best budget cadences

Klenty does the core SDR job, multichannel cadences with CRM sync, at prices that undercut everyone except Apollo's low tiers: Starter is about $50 per user per month (email only), Growth is $70 and adds multichannel plus CRM automation, and Plus is $99 with AI features and coaching. A parallel dialer is a $45 add-on.

The catch: at the $99 Plus tier you are close to negotiated Salesloft pricing while still missing the data layer, visitor identification, and the deeper analytics the bigger platforms carry. It is best when your list building already happens elsewhere.

How to choose the right platform

Match the tool to your team, not the other way around:

  • 1 to 15 reps, no ops team, need data too: Apollo. One login, one bill, free to test. This is where most of our clients start.
  • Enterprise team with RevOps and forecasting needs: Outreach or Salesloft; pick Salesloft if coaching matters most, Outreach for workflow depth.
  • Already paying for HubSpot or Salesforce: stay native with Sales Hub Professional or the Sales Engagement add-on before buying a standalone tool.
  • Want an AI SDR to run sequences for you: Reply.io with Jason AI, if the add-on math fits your pipeline value.
  • Tight budget, data handled elsewhere: Klenty Growth.

Whatever you pick, the platform is only half the system. Deliverability, list quality, and copy decide whether sequences book meetings, which is exactly what our cold email marketing service and lead generation service handle for clients end to end.

What changed since we first published this

We re-verified every platform's 2026 pricing on July 24, 2026. Two numbers needed correcting, and both make a vendor look better than our first version did:

  • Salesforce Sales Engagement is $50 per user per month, not $75. Salesforce's own pricing page now lists the add-on at $50 billed annually, so we lowered it in the table and the Salesforce section.
  • HubSpot Sales Hub no longer carries a five-seat minimum. HubSpot removed seat minimums for Sales Hub in 2024, so the real entry point for sequence features is one Professional seat at about $90 per month plus the one-time $1,500 onboarding, not the roughly $450 our first version implied.

Everything else held on re-check: Apollo is still Free to $119 per user per month, Klenty $50 to $99, and Reply.io $49 to $139 before add-ons, while Outreach and Salesloft remain quote-based. Our ranking is unchanged, because none of these updates change the core trade-off: only Apollo bundles the contact database, so it stays the best all-in-one value.

The verdict

Outreach and Salesloft remain excellent products, but in 2026 they are enterprise purchases: quote-based contracts, implementation fees, and a separate data bill. For everyone else, the value equation has shifted. Apollo delivers 80 percent of the engagement capability plus the database the others make you buy separately, at roughly a third of the cost, and you can validate it on a free plan this afternoon.
