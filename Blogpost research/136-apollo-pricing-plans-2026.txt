# Apollo.io Pricing (2026): Plans, Credits & Hidden Costs

Apollo raised the prices again. Most platforms do when they hit product-market fit, and Apollo's 2026 pricing reflects their market position. But understanding the credit system separates real value from hidden costs.

## Apollo.io Plans at a Glance

Apollo operates on a credit-based model where you buy credits monthly and spend them on contacts, emails, and account audits.

**Basic Plan**: $49/month
- 1,000 credits monthly
- Email outreach on Apollo
- Task management
- Email validation API access
- Limited integrations

**Professional Plan**: $199/month
- 5,000 credits
- Everything in Basic
- Zapier/n8n integration
- API access
- Priority support

**Organization Plan**: $499/month
- 15,000 credits
- Team seats
- API quotas increase
- Dedicated support
- Advanced filtering

**Enterprise Plan**: Custom pricing
- Custom credits
- White-label options
- SLAs guaranteed
- Technical onboarding

The catch? Credits don't map 1:1 to cost-per-lead.

## Understanding Apollo Credits Economics

This is where Apollo gets tricky. Credits are the currency, but different actions cost different amounts.

**Contact Export**: 1 credit per contact
- You're buying verified B2B contacts
- Pricing works out to approximately $0.05-0.10 per contact at the Pro plan
- Basic plan: roughly $0.049 per contact

**Email Sends**: 0.5 credits per email on Apollo's native outreach
- You're not emailing externally
- Tracking is Apollo-only
- Limited to Apollo's infrastructure

**Email Sequences**: Complex pricing
- Setting up automated sequences costs nothing
- But you're limited to Apollo's send limits
- Dependent on plan tier

**Account Audits**: 10 credits per audit
- These verify email deliverability
- Only useful if you're using Apollo's email
- Waste of credits if you're on a private server

**Email Validation API**: 1 credit per validation
- Standalone use cases
- Real value if you have dirty lists

Here's the math: A 5,000 credit Professional plan costs $199/month. If you export 4,000 contacts and validate 500 emails, you've spent all credits before sending a single campaign.

## What Apollo Doesn't Tell You

Apollo's pricing is built for **contact sourcing**, not cold email at scale.

If your workflow is:
1. Find contacts → 4,000 credits
2. Validate emails → 500 credits
3. Send outreach → 500 credits
4. Track replies

...you need the $499 plan minimum ($6,000/year).

Our clients who use Apollo typically use it for contact research, then export to their own email infrastructure. That's the honest use case.

**Private Server Alternative**: $489/year for 50 inboxes with Apollo contact data = $978 total first year. This gives you complete email control and no per-contact fees.

**Apollo Alone**: $199 × 12 = $2,388/year, plus additional credits for contact validation and audits. Realistic cost is $3,000-4,000/year for active prospectors.

## Apollo vs SmartLead Credit Pricing

SmartLead charges per seat, not per action:

- SmartLead: $99/month per user (unlimited contacts, emails, sequences)
- Apollo Pro: $199/month + overage credits

If you're doing volume outreach, SmartLead's model is cheaper. You're paying for the tool, not for data and actions within the tool.

Apollo wins if you need verified B2B contact data and only run occasional campaigns.

## The Verification Game

Apollo's big selling point is verification. They claim 95%+ accuracy on emails.

Real talk: Verification only matters if Apollo is also sending your emails. If you're exporting contacts to another tool (like Instantly or SmartLead), you're paying for verification you might not need.

Many of our clients skip the account audit credits entirely and rely on warmup with their private server instead. Lower upfront cost, same deliverability after warmup.

## Hidden Costs of Apollo Outreach

Apollo's native email sending has limitations:

**Sender Reputation**: Apollo sends from shared infrastructure. Your domain doesn't get warmed, so replies can drop to 40-50% without warmup.

**Tracking Limits**: Apollo's tracking is campaign-level, not prospect-level. You don't get the granular data that third-party ESP tracking provides.

**Sequence Limitations**: Apollo sequences are basic. No conditional logic, no dynamic content based on behavior.

If you use Apollo for outreach instead of contact sourcing, you're basically paying $2,388+/year for a mediocre email tool.

## ROI on Apollo Pricing

Let's assume you run a 5,000 contact campaign:

**Apollo-Only Model**:

The private server model wins financially after 3-4 campaigns when you're doing volume work.

## Apollo Pricing: Best Use Case

Apollo is best positioned as a **contact database tool**, not a complete cold email solution.

**Use Apollo for**:
- Researching verified B2B contacts
- Building prospect lists
- Email validation on cold lists
- Account-based marketing research

**Don't use Apollo for**:
- Your entire cold email operation
- High-volume outreach (use SmartLead or Instantly instead)
- Email warmup (unnecessary credits)
- Complex sequences (too basic)

## Apollo Credit Economics: The Real Cost Per Lead

Apollo's pricing revolves around credits. Understanding credit economics is essential before choosing a plan.

Each email credit gives you one email address. Each mobile credit gives you one phone number. The cost per credit drops as you move up tiers:

| Plan | Email Credits | Cost Per Email Credit | Mobile Credits | Cost Per Mobile Credit |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Free | 10,000/year | $0.00 | 5/month | $0.00 |
| Basic ($49/mo) | Unlimited | $0.00 | 75/month | $0.65 |
| Professional ($79/mo) | Unlimited | $0.00 | 100/month | $0.79 |
| Organization ($119/mo) | Unlimited | $0.00 | 200/month | $0.60 |

Email credits are unlimited on all paid plans. That's Apollo's biggest advantage over competitors like ZoomInfo ($15K+/year) and Cognism. For cold email campaigns, unlimited email lookups means you can build lists without worrying about credit caps.

Mobile credits matter if you're combining cold email with cold calling. At the Organization tier, 200 mobile credits/month costs $0.60 each — reasonable for a multi-channel approach. But if you only do email outreach, you'll never touch the mobile credits, and the Basic plan at $49/month gives you everything you need.
