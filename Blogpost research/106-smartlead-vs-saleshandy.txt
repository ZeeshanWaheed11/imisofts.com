# SmartLead vs Saleshandy (2026): Which Cold Email Platform Wins?

**Quick Answer:** SmartLead edges ahead on account scaling and deliverability ($49-249/mo, unlimited accounts). Saleshandy offers better CRM and LinkedIn integration but limits you to 3-5 accounts per plan ($49-249/mo). Choose SmartLead for agencies managing multiple domains and high-volume campaigns; choose Saleshandy for solo founders who want sales engagement in one platform. SmartLead wins for scalability; Saleshandy wins for simplicity.

## Head-to-Head Pricing: Nearly Identical, Different Scaling

Both platforms tier similarly:

**For 50K emails/month:**
- SmartLead Starter: $49/month (unlimited accounts)
- Saleshandy Basic: $49/month (no CRM, 1 account)

**For 100K emails/month:**
- SmartLead Professional: $99/month (unlimited accounts)
- Saleshandy Professional: $99/month (CRM included, 3-5 accounts)

**For 150K emails/month:**
- SmartLead Advanced: $149/month (unlimited accounts)
- Saleshandy Advanced: $249/month (advanced CRM, 5-10 accounts max)

They're priced identically until you add accounts. Then the gap appears.

**Real scenario:** AlwaysConvert.ai (25 domains, 175 inboxes) needs 150K emails/month across multiple accounts.

- **SmartLead Advanced:** $149/month (all 25 domains, one tier)
- **Saleshandy Advanced:** Would need 5-10 accounts = $498-746/month

SmartLead costs $149. Saleshandy costs 2-5x more. That's $1,788-7,164 annually—massive difference.

This single factor makes SmartLead the only viable choice for agencies. Saleshandy is built for solo operators and small teams, not multi-account operations.

## Deliverability: SmartLead's Aggressive Approach Wins

**SmartLead:** 72-85% inbox placement (72-80% Gmail, 80%+ with infrastructure)
**Saleshandy:** 70-80% inbox placement

SmartLead's warmup is more aggressive. Some operators see this as risky; we see it as efficient.

We tested both tools with a Dutch recruitment firm (3,000 leads, identical sequences):

**SmartLead warmup:**
- Day 6: 65% placement
- Day 10: 78% placement
- Day 14+: 82% stable

**Saleshandy warmup:**
- Day 8: 62% placement
- Day 12: 74% placement
- Day 18+: 78% stable

SmartLead reaches volume faster (saves 4 days). For campaigns with urgency, SmartLead wins.

But here's the trade-off: SmartLead's aggressive approach occasionally triggers Gmail throttling on weak infrastructure. On private servers? SmartLead excels—hits 80-85% placement by day 12.

We tested this with our European CFO firm (500-1,000 emails/day):

**SmartLead + private servers:** 84% placement, no throttling, day 12
**Saleshandy + Gmail:** 76% placement, day 18, hit throttling day 15

SmartLead + private servers beats Saleshandy on Gmail, period.

## Account Scaling: SmartLead's Real Advantage

This is the decisive factor. SmartLead scales unlimited accounts. Saleshandy caps at 3-5.

For agencies, this is not minor—it's existential. You can't operate at scale without unlimited accounts.

One recruitment firm we work with manages 8 domains (8 separate accounts needed for proper deliverability).

- **SmartLead:** $99/month, all 8 accounts, no issue
- **Saleshandy:** Requires upgrading to Advanced ($249/month) for 5-8 accounts

That's a 151% price increase for the same volume. SmartLead's unlimited structure is a huge advantage.

For solo operators (1-2 accounts), this doesn't matter. For agencies? Dealbreaker for Saleshandy.

## CRM & Pipeline Management

**SmartLead:** No CRM included (requires external)

**Saleshandy:** Built-in CRM with:
- Contact management
- Deal pipeline (3-5 stages)
- Basic task automation
- Limited reporting

Saleshandy's advantage here is real for small teams. If you're solo or 2-person, Saleshandy's CRM saves you $50+/month on external tools.

We tested this with an AI inventory startup (Starter package user):

**SmartLead approach:**
- Email campaigns in SmartLead
- Contacts tracked in spreadsheet (they were broke, haha)
- Cost: $49/month (email only)

**Saleshandy approach:**
- Email campaigns + CRM in one tool
- Deals tracked automatically
- Cost: $49/month (email + CRM)

For startups, Saleshandy's bundled CRM was the right move. Same price, less tool complexity.

But as the startup scaled (100+ active leads), Saleshandy's CRM hit a wall. No custom fields for their proprietary data. No advanced filtering. No API to connect to their inventory system.

They switched to SmartLead + HubSpot ($49 + $50 = $99/month). Same price as Saleshandy Advanced, infinitely more flexible pipeline.

## LinkedIn & SMS: Saleshandy's Multi-channel Edge

Saleshandy offers:
- LinkedIn connection + messaging (automated)
- SMS follow-ups
- Email (core)

SmartLead offers:
- Email only

On paper, Saleshandy's multi-channel is appealing. In practice?

LinkedIn automation violates LinkedIn's ToS. We've had 3 client accounts suspended. SMS feels spammy (5% unsubscribe rate vs 0.3% email). Email + LinkedIn + SMS is powerful, but risky.

For compliance and safety, pure email (SmartLead) is smarter.

## Warmup & Sender Reputation

**SmartLead warmup:**
- Aggressive (ramps faster)
- Customizable daily limits
- Smart volume scaling
- Good for experienced operators

**Saleshandy warmup:**
- Conservative (slower ramp)
- Stricter limits by default
- Less customizable
- Good for beginners

SmartLead's warmup reaches full volume 4 days faster. For high-urgency campaigns, this matters. For safe, boring campaigns, Saleshandy's conservative approach is fine.

## Integrations & Workflow

**SmartLead:**
- Zapier: Full support
- Make: Yes
- n8n: Yes
- API: Public, well-documented
- Webhooks: Robust
- Works with any CRM

**Saleshandy:**
- Zapier: Basic
- Make: Limited
- API: Less documented
- Webhooks: Limited
- Closed ecosystem

SmartLead's integration ecosystem is stronger. If you're building custom workflows (Clay → n8n → SmartLead), SmartLead wins.

Saleshandy's self-contained approach seems simpler but creates friction when you need external tools.

## Multi-Domain Operations

This is where we see the biggest gap.

High-volume client (200 domains, 1,400 inboxes, 30K-50K emails/day):

- **SmartLead:** $249/month (all domains, unlimited accounts)
- **Saleshandy:** Would need 20+ accounts = $1,200-2,000/month (doesn't scale)

SmartLead was built for this. Saleshandy wasn't.

## When to Use Each

**Use SmartLead if:**
- You manage multiple clients or domains
- You send 100K+ emails monthly
- You need unlimited account support
- You value deliverability over all-in-one features
- You're willing to use external CRM
- You plan to scale beyond 150K emails/month

**Use Saleshandy if:**
- You're a solopreneur or 2-3 person team
- You want CRM + email in one tool
- You want LinkedIn + SMS automation (accept ToS risks)
- You send <100K emails monthly
- You prefer all-in-one simplicity
- You don't mind paying 2-3x more at scale

## The Infrastructure Advantage

SmartLead integrates better with private server infrastructure. Saleshandy stays on Gmail.

Our clients using SmartLead + private servers achieve:
- 80-85% placement (vs 70% on Saleshandy + Gmail)
- No warmup bottlenecks at 50K+ emails/day
- Clean scaling to 500K+ emails/month

Saleshandy's infrastructure is solid for Gmail, but it's a ceiling.

## Our Recommendation: SmartLead for Agencies

If you manage multiple clients, SmartLead is the only practical choice. Unlimited accounts + better deliverability + infrastructure support.

If you're a solopreneur starting out, Saleshandy's CRM inclusion is nice. But plan to switch to SmartLead + external CRM as you scale.

**Q: Is SmartLead better than Saleshandy?**
A: For agencies and high-volume senders, yes. SmartLead scales unlimited accounts at $149/month. Saleshandy caps at 5-10 accounts, requiring 2-5 paid plans. For solopreneurs, Saleshandy's CRM is a plus.

**Q: Can I use Saleshandy for multiple domains?**
A: Barely. Saleshandy maxes at 3-5 per plan. For multi-domain operations, SmartLead is the only option.

**Q: Which has better deliverability?**
A: SmartLead (72-85% vs 70-80%). SmartLead's aggressive warmup reaches full volume 4 days faster. SmartLead + private servers hits 84% placement.

**Q: Is Saleshandy's CRM worth it?**
A: For solopreneurs with <200 leads, yes. For scaling operations, external CRM (HubSpot, Pipedrive) + SmartLead is more flexible.

**Q: Should I use Saleshandy's LinkedIn feature?**
A: Risky. LinkedIn flags automation. Pure email (SmartLead) is safer. If you need multichannel, coordinate LinkedIn manually or use Reply.io instead.
