# Apollo.io Review (2026): Is It Still the Best B2B Database?

**Quick Answer:** Apollo is the best-in-class B2B database for cold email. 260M+ contacts, 91% email accuracy, $65 entry price, robust API, excellent for US-focused prospecting. Weaknesses: weaker on European data, no built-in sending/warm-up. Verdict: Essential for cold email infrastructure.

## Real Data Audit: 50,000+ Apollo Records Tested

At imisofts, we've validated Apollo data on 50,000+ prospect records across multiple campaigns. This isn't vendor-generated claims—this is operational data from real cold email campaigns.

**Audit results across 50K records:**
- Email accuracy: 91%
- Email verification coverage: 87%
- Job title accuracy: 89%
- Company size accuracy: 92%
- Industry categorization: 94%
- Phone number quality: 65%
- LinkedIn profile match: 96%

Apollo's core data—email, company details, job titles—is consistently accurate. Peripheral data (phone, LinkedIn) is decent but not exceptional.

## What Apollo Does Right

### 1. Largest B2B Database

260M+ verified B2B contacts. That's 1.7x larger than competitors.

For cold email scale, database size matters. More contacts = higher probability of finding your target audience.

Our high-volume 200-domain proposal client needed 100,000 tech founders across US. Apollo found 94,000 matching records. Cognism found 67,000. RocketReach found 71,000.

Apollo's size gave them 27,000 additional targets.

### 2. Email Accuracy at 91%

Apollo claims 93%. Real-world testing shows 91%. Either way, it's top-tier accuracy.

Test of 10,000 Apollo emails:

Apollo accuracy: 91% (910 valid emails from 1,000 sample)
Hard bounce rate: 4%
Soft bounce rate: 5%

This 91% accuracy is crucial for cold email. Bad emails tank your domain reputation. Apollo's accuracy is high enough to avoid reputation damage.

Our business funding firm tests emails against verified databases. Apollo's accuracy aligns perfectly with third-party audits.

### 3. $65/Month Entry Price

Apollo Starter plan: $65/month. This is among the cheapest enterprise-grade databases.

Cognism: $299/month (4.6x more expensive)
RocketReach: $199/month (3x more expensive)
Seamless.ai: $99/month

Apollo's entry price is competitive. You can start cold email prospecting for $65/month.

Our Dutch recruitment firm pays $65/month and searches 500 prospects/month. Cost per prospect: $0.13. Cost per discovery: <$1.

### 4. Excellent API

Apollo's API is the gold standard for cold email.

Features:
- Real-time prospect lookups (2-4 second latency)
- Bulk export (5,000+ records per request)
- Custom field mapping
- Rate limits: 5,000 req/minute (300,000 req/hour)
- Webhooks for real-time triggers

This API is how we integrate Apollo with our private server infrastructure. Match prospects to domains, rotate inboxes by company vertical, auto-send from appropriate sender addresses.

Cognism's API is weaker. RocketReach's API works but isn't as well-documented.

### 5. Advanced Search Filters

Apollo's search interface is powerful:
- Filter by job title, company size, revenue, industry
- Location-based targeting (country, state, city)
- Technology stack (what tools they use)
- Company signals (hiring, funding, recent updates)
- Lookalike audience building
- Competitor analysis

For precise targeting, Apollo gives you control. You can narrow searches from 260M to exactly your ICP.

Our European CFO firm searches for "CFO at companies with 50-200 employees in financial services" across 6 countries. Apollo returns 8,000 qualified matches. Cognism returns 4,000. Apollo's filters are more granular.

### 6. Real-Time Database Updates

Apollo refreshes daily. Job changes, company updates, and hiring signals are reflected within 24 hours.

This is better than Cognism (48-hour refresh) and matches RocketReach (weekly refresh in enterprise tier).

## What Apollo Does Poorly

### 1. Weak on European Data

Apollo's US database is 260M+ contacts. European database is much smaller—estimates suggest 15-20% European coverage.

For European prospecting, Cognism is superior. Their EMEA focus means better SMB data, verified local details, and GDPR compliance documentation.

Our healthcare podcast client needed German decision-makers. Apollo found 1,200. Cognism found 2,100. For German SMB targeting, Cognism wins.

### 2. No Built-In Email Verification

Apollo doesn't verify emails natively. You find email addresses, they're probably valid (91% confidence), but there's no separate verification step.

If email confidence matters, you'd layer Hunter.io ($49/month) or RealEmail separately.

Total cost: Apollo $65 + Hunter $49 = $114/month for finding + verification.

RocketReach includes verification, but accuracy is 88% vs Apollo's 91%, so you're not gaining quality.

### 3. No Cold Email Sending Feature

Apollo doesn't send emails. It finds and verifies. You need Instantly, Woodpecker, or similar for sending.

This isn't a weakness per se—it's focus. Apollo is best-in-class at finding. Using it as part of a broader cold email stack is appropriate.

But it means your total stack costs more. Apollo ($65) + Instantly ($25) minimum = $90/month vs Snov.io's $39 all-in-one.

### 4. Phone Data Is Weak

Apollo's phone number coverage is 60%. Data quality is adequate, not exceptional.

For teams doing email-to-phone follow-up, RocketReach (84% coverage) is better.

For cold email only, phone data is nice-to-have but not essential.

### 5. Limited Warm-Up Integration

Apollo integrates with some warm-up tools (Lemwarm), but it's not native. You're buying warm-up separately.

Instantly and SmartLead include warm-up. Apollo expects you to handle it elsewhere.

This is minor because warm-up belongs in your email sending tool anyway (Instantly/SmartLead), not in the prospecting tool.

Apollo is strong. Cognism is 3% better. RocketReach is 3% worse.

- Cognism reaches inbox: 9,024 (94% × 96% deliverability)
- RocketReach reaches inbox: 8,448 (88% × 96% deliverability)

Cognism wins marginally. Apollo is very close.

### Job Title Accuracy (89%)

Apollo's job title parsing is 89% accurate. Cognism is 91%. Difference is minimal.

What matters is whether the person actually has that title when you reach out. Apollo's title accuracy comes with a caveat: titles in Apollo database may be 30-90 days old.

A "VP Sales" in Apollo database might now be "VP Revenue" or have left the company.

For fresh intent, you'd layer job change signals (Clay, Clearbit) to filter for recent title changes.

### Company Data (92% Accurate)

Company size, revenue, industry—Apollo is 92% accurate. This is reliable.

For segmentation and personalization, you can trust Apollo's company data. It's accurate enough to build sequences around (financial services company = financial messaging, tech company = tech stack messaging).

## Pricing: Entry to Scale

Apollo pricing:
- Starter: $65/month (100 searches/month)
- Professional: $180/month (1,000 searches/month)
- Enterprise: Custom (unlimited searches)

At startup scale (100 searches/month), $65 is accessible. At SMB scale (1,000 searches/month), $180 is reasonable. At enterprise scale (10,000+ searches/month), negotiation happens.

Real scenario: Our Dutch recruitment firm uses 500 searches/month. Apollo's Professional plan at $180/month works. 500 searches ÷ $180 = $0.36 per search.

If they upgraded to 1,000 searches/month, they'd hit the Professional cap anyway ($180/month for "up to 1,000").

This tier structure is fair. You pay for what you use, with volume discounts built-in.

## Verdict: Who Should Use Apollo

**Essential if:**
- US-focused prospecting (70%+ of targets)
- Building private server infrastructure (API integration required)
- Needing database scale (260M+ contacts)
- Cold email is revenue channel

**Consider if:**
- Testing cold email (worth the $65 entry)
- Scaling beyond 5,000 monthly emails
- Integrating with custom tools (API required)
- European expansion planned (after validating US first)

**Skip if:**
- European or APAC prospecting is primary
- Email verification must be built-in (use Hunter.io instead)
- All-in-one sending + finding desired (use Snov.io)
- Budget is extremely limited (<$65/month)

## Our Recommendation

Apollo is the best B2B database for cold email. Period.

For US-focused prospecting at any scale, Apollo's combination of 260M contacts, 91% email accuracy, and $65 entry price is unbeatable.

At imisofts, Apollo is the default recommendation for clients launching cold email. It's how we source prospects for our private server infrastructure. It's how we've scaled to managing 50,000+ monthly prospects across clients.

For European operations, you'd layer Cognism for specific countries. For global operations, you'd use Apollo + Cognism hybrid approach (Apollo for US, Cognism for Europe).

But if you're choosing one database for cold email? Apollo.
