# Instantly vs Saleshandy (2026): Full Comparison With Real Data

**Quick Answer:** Instantly dominates on price and scalability ($29-99/month), while Saleshandy offers better sales engagement features ($49-249/month). For pure cold email volume, Instantly wins. For integrated CRM functionality and deal tracking, Saleshandy wins. Choose Instantly if you're running 100K+ emails monthly across multiple clients; choose Saleshandy if you need end-to-end sales management within one tool.

## The Real Cost Breakdown

We manage both tools daily across our client accounts. Here's what you actually pay:

**Instantly:**
- Starter ($29/mo): 30K emails, 1 account
- Professional ($79/mo): 100K emails, unlimited accounts
- Growth ($99/mo): 250K emails, unlimited everything

**Saleshandy:**
- Basic ($49/mo): 25K emails, no CRM
- Professional ($99/mo): 100K emails, CRM included
- Advanced ($249/mo): 200K emails, advanced CRM + SMS

For a 100K email/month operation:
- **Instantly:** $79 (unlimited accounts)
- **Saleshandy:** $99 (3-5 accounts, CRM included)

The $20 difference seems small until you factor in accounts. Saleshandy limits you to 3-5 accounts per plan tier. Instantly? Unlimited. For AlwaysConvert.ai (175 inboxes across 25 domains), Instantly costs $79/month with zero restrictions. Saleshandy would require two or three paid workspaces—pushing costs to $198-297/month.

**Real scenario:** One recruitment firm we work with started on Saleshandy's Professional plan ($99/mo) to manage 3 domains. Six months later, they added 4 more domains. Saleshandy required an upgrade to Advanced ($249/mo). We migrated them to Instantly Professional ($79/mo)—one tier, unlimited domains. They saved $170/month (50% reduction).

## Deliverability: Nearly Identical, Different Warmup Strategies

Both tools use Gmail API by default. In our testing, placement rates are nearly identical.

**Instantly:**
- 72% inbox placement (Gmail infrastructure)
- 80%+ with private server integration
- Warmup: 8-10 days to full volume
- Conservative approach (protects reputation aggressively)

**Saleshandy:**
- 70% inbox placement (Gmail infrastructure)
- Warmup: 7-9 days to full volume
- Slightly more aggressive (can hit limits faster)

We tested both with a healthcare podcast client (previously blacklisted on 4 domains with Gmail). Results:

**Instantly + private servers:** 84% placement after 10-day warmup
**Saleshandy + Gmail:** 68% placement, but hit Gmail throttling on day 12

Saleshandy can be faster to ramp but less resilient at scale. Instantly ramps slower but holds placement better long-term.

## Features: Email vs Sales Engagement

This is where the tools diverge sharply.

### Instantly: Pure Email Focus

Instantly does one thing: send cold emails reliably. No CRM, no pipeline, no sales complexity. You integrate it with your CRM separately via Zapier or API.

**Advantages:**
- Faster setup (2-3 hours to first campaign)
- Lighter dashboard (less cognitive load)
- Better email deliverability (no bloat)
- Compatible with any CRM

**Disadvantages:**
- Zero pipeline tracking
- No sales engagement beyond email
- Requires external CRM
- Limited analytics

### Saleshandy: Email + Sales Engagement

Saleshandy is email + CRM + LinkedIn + SMS in one platform. You get a built-in sales pipeline, deal tracking, and engagement automation.

**Advantages:**
- Complete sales stack in one tool
- CRM included (no extra software)
- LinkedIn outreach built-in
- SMS follow-ups
- Better for solo founders/small teams

**Disadvantages:**
- CRM is lighter than dedicated tools (HubSpot, Pipedrive)
- LinkedIn feature relies on web scraping (risks account suspension)
- Costs more per inbox/email
- Harder to customize at scale

## Multi-Channel Outreach: Saleshandy Wins

Saleshandy's secret advantage is that it's not just email.

After your initial cold email, Saleshandy can:
1. Wait 3 days
2. Send an automated LinkedIn connection + message
3. Wait 2 days
4. Send SMS follow-up
5. Wait 1 day
6. Email again

This integrated sequence is genuinely powerful for outbound. Instantly? Email only. You'd need to manually coordinate LinkedIn in Dex or n8n.

We tested this with a European CFO firm (500-1,000 emails/day across 6 countries). Saleshandy's multi-channel sequences delivered:
- Email reply rate: 2.1%
- Email → LinkedIn → SMS → Email sequence: 3.4% reply rate
- Conversions worth extra effort: Yes

But here's the catch: the LinkedIn feature uses automation that violates LinkedIn's ToS. Two clients saw accounts suspended after heavy SMS + LinkedIn automation. LinkedIn is flagging these patterns.

Instantly doesn't have this problem because Instantly sticks to email. Safer at scale.

## CRM & Pipeline: Saleshandy's Built-in Edge

Saleshandy includes a CRM. It's not Pipedrive, but it works.

Features:
- Contact management
- Deal tracking
- Basic pipeline (3-5 stages)
- Task automation
- Simple reporting

For solo founders or small teams (2-5 people), this is enough. You send cold emails, track replies in one dashboard, move deals through pipeline.

Instantly requires you to use external CRM. That's actually better for agencies (more flexibility), but worse for small teams (more tools to manage).

**Real scenario:** A US-based AI inventory startup we work with started on Instantly with a spreadsheet-based pipeline. After 30 days, they switched to Saleshandy for the built-in CRM. They now track 500 leads at various pipeline stages without leaving one platform. Cost: $99/month for email + CRM. If they'd stayed with Instantly + added HubSpot, they'd pay $79 + $50 = $129/month (and more complexity).

## Warmup & Sender Reputation

Both tools have solid warmup.

**Instantly warmup:**
- Automated: Sends small batches first, scales gradually
- Customizable: You set daily limits
- Duration: 8-10 days to full volume
- Free: 50K warmup emails included

**Saleshandy warmup:**
- Automated: Slightly more aggressive
- Customizable: Less granular control
- Duration: 7-9 days
- Free: Limited warmup (needs upgrade for more)

Winner: Tie. Both are reliable. Instantly gives you more control; Saleshandy is more aggressive.

## Integrations & Automation

**Instantly:**
- Zapier: Yes
- Make: Yes
- n8n: Yes
- API: Public API available
- Slack: Yes
- Integrates with ANY CRM via webhook

**Saleshandy:**
- Built-in CRM (no external needed)
- Limited external integrations
- Zapier: Basic only
- API: Exists but less documented
- More closed-system approach

If you have custom workflows (Clay → n8n → Instantly), Instantly's API is superior. If you want everything in one place, Saleshandy wins.
