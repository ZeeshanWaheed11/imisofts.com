# Apollo Search Filters: Find Decision-Makers with Precision Targeting

Apollo is the #1 B2B database (10M+ contacts worldwide). Most users use 10% of its power.

This guide shows how to use Apollo's advanced filters to find exactly your target prospect in 5 minutes.

## Apollo Basics: How It Works

1. Create Apollo account (free tier: 10 searches/month)
2. Search → Add Filter
3. Combine filters (title + company size + country + technology)
4. Apollo returns matching contacts
5. Export as CSV → Upload to Instantly/SmartLead

**Expected Results:**
- Focused search: 2,000-5,000 contacts
- Broad search: 10,000-25,000 contacts
- Quality: 60-70% accurate emails

## Key Filters (With Examples)

### Filter 1: Territory/Location

**Use Case:** Target specific country or region.

**Examples:**
- Country = United Kingdom (returns all UK contacts)
- Country = Germany (returns German B2B only)
- City = London (returns London-based only)
- Region = California (for US)

**Tip:** Combine with other filters to narrow. "Country = UK" alone returns 50K+ contacts (too broad).

### Filter 2: Job Title

This is the most important filter. Get it right.

**Decision-Maker Titles by Vertical:**

**SaaS/Tech:**
- VP Growth
- Head of Sales
- Director of Business Development
- Founder
- Chief Revenue Officer

**Fintech:**
- Head of Partnerships
- VP Growth
- Director of Strategy
- Chief Product Officer

**Agencies:**
- Founder
- Managing Director
- VP Business Development
- Director of New Business

**Enterprise Software:**
- VP Sales
- Head of Enterprise
- Director of Sales
- VP Partnerships

**How to Use:**
1. Filters → Job Title
2. Search for title (e.g., "VP Growth")
3. Apollo suggests matching titles
4. Select multiple if needed (VP Growth OR Head of Sales)

**Pro Tip:** Don't select "Sales Manager" or "Sales Rep." Too junior. Target Director+ level.

### Filter 3: Company Size

Different verticals need different company sizes.

**Company Size Definitions (Apollo):**
- 1-10: Seed-stage startups
- 11-50: Early-stage (Series A)
- 51-200: Growth-stage
- 201-500: Series B/C
- 501-1000: Late-stage
- 1000+: Enterprise

**When to Use:**
- **SaaS:** 10-500 employees (highest response)
- **Agencies:** 10-100 employees (small to mid)
- **Enterprise Software:** 500+ employees (longer sales cycle, higher deal value)
- **Fintech:** 50-500 employees (growth + profitability)

**How to Filter:**
1. Filters → Company Size
2. Select range (e.g., 51-200)
3. Apollo filters results

### Filter 4: Company Industry

Narrow by industry vertical.

**Common Industries:**
- Software / SaaS
- Fintech / Financial Services
- Marketing / Advertising
- Staffing / Recruitment
- Logistics / Supply Chain
- Healthcare Technology
- HR Technology

**How to Use:**
1. Filters → Industry
2. Type or select from list
3. Apollo returns matching companies + contacts

**Pro Tip:** Too broad (e.g., "Technology") returns millions. Go specific ("SaaS" or "HR Technology").

### Filter 5: Company Funding

Find well-funded companies (more budget = better buyers).

**Funding Filters:**
- Last Funding: 0-6 months ago (highest intent)
- Funding Stage: Seed, Series A, Series B, Series C+
- Funding Amount: $1M-10M, $10M-50M, $50M+

**Use Case:** Target companies that raised funding recently = high growth = budget available.

**Example Search:**
- Industry: SaaS
- Country: UK
- Funding Stage: Series A or Series B
- Last Funding: Within 6 months
- Title: VP Growth

**Result:** ~500-2,000 highly-qualified UK SaaS companies that just raised funding.

### Filter 6: Technologies Used

Find companies using specific tech (shows buying intent + budget).

**Common Technologies:**
- Salesforce (using CRM = has revenue team)
- HubSpot (has marketing/sales operations)
- Stripe (handling payments = profitable)
- AWS (infrastructure = tech-forward)
- Intercom (customer communication = growth-focused)

**Use Case:** Companies using Salesforce have revenue teams = higher propensity to buy sales tools.

**How to Filter:**
1. Filters → Technology
2. Type technology (e.g., "Salesforce")
3. Apollo returns companies using it

**Advanced Use:**
- Salesforce + HubSpot = highly mature sales team
- Stripe + Intercom = profitable, customer-focused SaaS
- AWS + Datadog = engineering-forward startup

## Pro Tips for Apollo Searches

### Tip 1: Use "OR" for Job Title

Don't search "VP Growth" alone. Use "VP Growth OR Head of Sales OR Director Growth" to capture variations.

### Tip 2: Don't Over-Filter

Each filter narrows results:
- Country = 50K+
- Country + Industry = 10K+
- Country + Industry + Size = 5K+
- Country + Industry + Size + Title = 1K-3K

Stop when results are 1K-5K (sweet spot for manageable campaigns).

### Tip 3: Export and Test

Export 500-1,000 from search as CSV. Test in Instantly/SmartLead with small pilot (50/day). If response rate >3%, scale to full list.

### Tip 4: Technology Filter is Powerful

Companies using specific tools = buying intent. If searching "CRM buyers," filter for Salesforce OR HubSpot.

### Tip 5: Combine Funding + Recency

"Series A funded in last 6 months" = recent money = near-term buying.

## Common Mistakes in Apollo Searches

**Mistake 1: Too Broad**
- Search "Technology" industry alone = 500K+ results (unusable)
- Fix: Narrow to "SaaS" or "HR Technology"

**Mistake 2: Too Narrow**
- Search "VP Growth" + "Series B" + "London" = 10 results (too small)
- Fix: Remove "London," expand to "UK"

**Mistake 3: Junior Titles**
- Searching "Sales Manager" instead of "VP Sales" = wrong audience
- Fix: Target Director+ level

**Mistake 4: Wrong Geography**
- Searching "Global" (no country filter) = millions of results
- Fix: Pick 1-3 countries

**Mistake 5: No Technology Filter**
- Generic searches miss high-intent prospects
- Fix: Add "Salesforce" or "HubSpot" to find buyers
