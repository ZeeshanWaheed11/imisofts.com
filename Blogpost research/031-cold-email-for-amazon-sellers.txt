# Cold Email for Amazon Brand Automation: The n8n Workflow Guide

Amazon FBA sellers are a unique target: profitable, growth-focused, and actively seeking partners (3PLs, brand protection services, product developers, marketing services).

Cold email to Amazon sellers is high-ROI. The challenge: Amazon seller lists are messy. You need to identify sellers, research their products, find decision-makers, qualify them, then reach them at scale.

Manual process: 2 minutes per seller = 200 sellers = 400 minutes. That's not scalable.

Solution: Automate the entire pipeline with n8n workflows.

At imisofts, we worked with Johan (MontBlack Inc), who sells products to Amazon sellers. He built a 7-step automation workflow that:

1. Identifies profitable brands on Keepa
2. Researches brand owners
3. Finds email addresses on ZoomInfo
4. Stores qualified leads in Close CRM
5. Sends cold emails via Instantly
6. Tracks replies in Close
7. Qualifies conversations

Investment: $2,500 initial setup. Cost per lead: under $0.50. Payoff: 200-400 qualified conversations per month.

This post shares his exact workflow and how to build it.

## The Johan Workflow Architecture

Johan built a 7-step workflow:

**Step 1: Identify Brands (Keepa)**
- Use Keepa API to search profitable brands
- Filter: Sales rank improving, organic sales, low competition
- Export list: 500-1,000 brands per month

**Step 2: Brand Research (Manual or API)**
- Research brand owner/founder (website, LinkedIn, whois)
- Collect company name, founder name
- Estimate company size, location

**Step 3: Find Decision-Makers (ZoomInfo)**
- Use ZoomInfo API to search company
- Find founder/CEO/operations manager email
- Collect verified email address

**Step 4: Qualify Leads (n8n Logic)**
- Filter: Valid email, complete company data
- Tag: Industry, company size, location
- Store qualified leads only

**Step 5: Store in CRM (Close CRM)**
- Push qualified leads to Close
- Auto-tag by segment
- Set follow-up reminders

**Step 6: Send Emails (Instantly)**
- Trigger personalized email from Instantly
- Variable injection: {{company_name}}, {{founder_name}}, {{personalization}}
- Track open/click in Instantly

**Step 7: Track & Qualify (Close)**
- Log replies in Close
- Auto-tag as "replied," "qualified," "uninterested"
- Create next-step reminders for sales team

## Step-by-Step Workflow Breakdown

**Trigger**: Schedule (runs daily at 8am)

**Action 1**: Query Keepa API for high-potential brands
- Filter: Sales rank improving 30% in past 90 days
- Filter: Estimated monthly sales > $5,000
- Filter: Organic sales > 70%
- Limit: 50 brands per run

**Action 2**: For each brand, extract company info
- Website domain
- Founder/CEO name
- Company location

**Action 3**: Query ZoomInfo for company + email
- Search company name + domain
- Extract CEO/founder email
- Verify email format

**Action 4**: Validate and filter
- Email exists: ✓ Yes
- Company size 5-500 employees: ✓ Yes
- Relevance score > 80: ✓ Yes

**Action 5**: Create Close CRM record
- Company name
- Founder name
- Email address
- Data source (Keepa)
- Segment tag (by company size, location)

**Action 6**: Trigger Instantly email
- Template: Amazon seller outreach
- Variables: {{company_name}}, {{founder_name}}, {{personalization_line}}
- Sending account: Rotate through 5+ accounts for deliverability

**Action 7**: Create Close follow-up task
- Log email send
- Schedule follow-up in 3 days
- Trigger SMS/email reminder to sales team

## Cold Email Copy for Amazon Sellers

Email 1:

"Hi [Name],

I noticed [Brand Name] on Amazon. Nice growth trajectory—particularly impressive organic ranking.

Quick question: How are you currently handling [challenge—warehouse logistics, international expansion, PPC optimization, brand protection, etc.]?

## Common Amazon Seller Outreach Mistakes

Mistake 1: Poor list quality. If you're hitting the wrong sellers (low-performing, unprofitable), response rate tanks. Keepa filtering matters.

Mistake 2: Generic pitches. "We help Amazon sellers" doesn't work. "We help FBA sellers scale profitably through [specific service]" does.

Mistake 3: Not respecting seller time. Sellers are busy. Keep emails short. Make CTAs clear.

Mistake 4: Ignoring deliverability. If your infrastructure is poor, emails go to spam. Use imisofts or quality SMTP infrastructure.

Mistake 5: Not tracking automation quality. Monitor: List quality (% valid emails), email engagement (% open rate), conversion rate. Iterate based on data.
