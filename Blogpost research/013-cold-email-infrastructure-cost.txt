# Cold Email Infrastructure Cost: What You Should Actually Pay in 2026

How much should you actually spend on email infrastructure for cold outreach? The answer varies wildly depending on your approach.

We've analyzed pricing across all major infrastructure models in 2026, and the range is shocking: from $199/year to $15,000+/year for similar volumes. Most teams are overpaying by 10-30x.

At imisofts, we've built cost-efficient infrastructure that delivers better results than competitors costing 5x more. Let me break down the real numbers.

## The Four Cold Email Infrastructure Models

### Model 1: Google Workspace (Shared IP Infrastructure)

This is the default choice for most teams because it's familiar.

**Pricing: $6-14/user/month**
- 250 inboxes × $6/month = $1,500/month = $18,000/year (economy)
- 250 inboxes × $14/month = $3,500/month = $42,000/year (premium)
- Real annual cost: $18,000-42,000

**What You Get:**
- 250 Gmail inboxes
- Shared IP infrastructure
- Google's reputation (good and bad)
- Gmail interface
- Basic support

**Hidden Costs:**
- One problematic sender burns all 250 accounts
- Shared IP blacklist = everyone suffers
- No reputation isolation per domain
- No dedicated support for cold email use case

**Real-World Volume:** 2,000-3,000 emails/day (limited by Gmail's per-inbox sending limits)

**Verdict:** Cheapest upfront, most expensive when one shared IP gets flagged.

### Model 2: Outlook/Microsoft 365 (Shared IP Infrastructure)

Similar to Google Workspace but using Outlook infrastructure.

**Pricing: $6-22/user/month**
- 250 inboxes × $6/month = $1,500/month = $18,000/year
- 250 inboxes × $22/month = $5,500/month = $66,000/year (enterprise)
- Real annual cost: $18,000-66,000

**What You Get:**
- 250 Outlook inboxes
- Microsoft's infrastructure
- Outlook interface
- OneDrive, Teams integration
- Compliance certifications (good for enterprise buyers)

**Hidden Costs:**
- Stricter sending limits than Gmail
- Microsoft flagging aggressive cold email senders
- Shared IP reputation issues
- Slower recovery from blacklists

**Real-World Volume:** 1,500-2,000 emails/day (conservative due to Microsoft's policies)

**Verdict:** More restrictive than Google, similar cost, higher risk for cold email.

### Model 3: Platform-Hosted Infrastructure (Instantly, SmartLead, Mailforge)

Third-party platforms host the infrastructure for you, managed reputation, limited customization.

**Pricing: $4,500-15,000+/year**

**Instantly Example:**
- 250 inboxes: $40-50/month = $480-600/year (inboxes)
- Platform fees: $500-5,000/month depending on campaign volume
- Total annual: $6,500-65,000

**SmartLead Example:**
- Shared inboxes: $500-3,000/month = $6,000-36,000/year
- Campaign management tools included
- Total annual: $6,000-36,000

**What You Get:**
- Managed email infrastructure
- Reputation management (their team handles it)
- Built-in cold email tools
- Warmup included
- Technical support for email delivery

**Hidden Costs:**
- Per-seat pricing (expensive to scale)
- Platform lock-in
- Less control over DNS/reputation
- Monthly commitment, hard to pause
- Setup fees ($1,000-3,000 typically)

**Real-World Volume:** 3,000-5,000 emails/day

**Verdict:** Middle ground, good for teams who want hands-off, expensive long-term.
