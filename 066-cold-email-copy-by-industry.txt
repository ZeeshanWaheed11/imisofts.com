# Cold Email Copy by Industry: What Works in 15 Different Verticals

"Cold email copy that works in SaaS doesn't work in insurance. Insurance copy doesn't work in cleaning. Cleaning copy doesn't work in real estate."

This is the biggest lesson from our 50M+ cold emails across 15 different verticals.

Here's the framework for each.

## The Industry Copy Framework

Each industry has:
1. **Pain points** (what keeps them up at night)
2. **Success metrics** (what they measure)
3. **Decision-making timeline** (fast vs. slow)
4. **CTA preference** (what ask gets replies)
5. **Tone** (formal vs. conversational)

Get these right, and your reply rate triples. Get them wrong, and you blend into the spam folder.

## Industry 1: SaaS

**Pain points:** Pipeline generation, lengthy sales cycles, low conversion rates

**Success metrics:** Qualified meetings per month, CAC (customer acquisition cost), ARR (annual recurring revenue)

**Timeline:** Fast (decision in 2-4 weeks)

**CTA:** "Book a 15-min strategy call" or "Quick demo call"

**Tone:** Casual, data-driven, competitive

**Copy angle:** Lead generation speed + cost per acquisition

Example:

"Most SaaS founders spend 12-15 hours/week on manual prospecting. We help teams cut that to 3 hours using [approach]. One founder we worked with booked 12 qualified meetings in 30 days."

---

## Industry 2: Real Estate

**Pain points:** Lead generation cost, pipeline consistency, closing rates

**Success metrics:** Deals per month, cost per deal, total transaction volume

**Timeline:** Medium (decision in 2-6 weeks)

**CTA:** "Let's hop on a call—I have data on [market]"

**Tone:** Friendly, confident, market-focused

**Copy angle:** Cost per deal + specific market data

Example:

"Most real estate teams spend $2-5K/month on ads. We help teams book deals through cold outreach at $150-300 per deal instead. One agent in your market just closed 8 deals this quarter."

## How to Adapt Copy for Your Industry

1. **Identify your industry pain points** (what keeps them up at night?)
2. **Define their success metrics** (what do they measure?)
3. **Understand their timeline** (fast or slow decision-makers?)
4. **Match CTA to industry** (direct ask vs. soft ask?)
5. **Adopt the tone** (formal vs. casual?)
6. **Lead with relevant data** (competitor reference, metric improvement, timeline shortening)

## Cross-Industry Copy Principle

The best cold email copy:
- References their specific industry pain
- Mentions a relevant success metric
- Provides proof (case study, data, metric improvement)
- Asks for action in their preferred format

Get all four right, and reply rates triple across all verticals.

FAQ_SCHEMA:
Q: Does the same cold email copy work across all industries?
A: No. Different industries have different pain points, success metrics, and decision timelines. Copy that works in SaaS fails in insurance. Always match copy to industry.

Q: What's the most important part of industry-specific copy?
A: Leading with a relevant pain point or success metric. Mention what they actually measure and care about, not generic benefits.

Q: How do I know if my copy is industry-specific enough?
A: Ask yourself: Could this email be sent to five other industries? If yes, it's too generic. If no, you're on the right track.

Q: Should my CTA change by industry?
A: Absolutely. Healthcare prefers phone CTAs. SaaS prefers calendar links. Podcasts prefer guest interview asks. Match CTA to industry preference.

Q: What's the best way to research industry-specific pain points?
A: Talk to 5-10 people in your target industry. Ask: "What's your biggest challenge?" Their answers are gold. Use those exact phrases in your copy.
